# WORK ORDER 017 - Bracing for Impact: The Defensive Half of the Charge
*Project: FORMATIONS - Phase 2 - Issued by Technical Director*
*GREEN LIGHT: proceed immediately upon reading. Escalate only per governance triggers.*
*Design authority: DESIGN_RULINGS_v1.2 R15 + R16 (new, below). Base: WO-016c branch state.*

## Context (why this WO exists)
WO-016c implemented directional charge shock exactly to spec. Reviewing the resulting spectrum, the TD finds the model incomplete, not incorrect: shock is a flat subtraction, so with base_shock 76.68 **every full-gallop frontal charge leaves fresh infantry wavering (<=30) and routs any unit below ~87 cohesion**. There is no "the line holds" outcome at any cohesion value, and flank/rear multipliers add little because the front is already lethal. The missing piece is the DEFENSIVE half - infantry bracing for impact. Do NOT retune the coefficient in isolation; build the brace, then tune the whole spectrum once.

## RULING R16 - Three-tier brace (append to DESIGN_RULINGS_v1.2)
Charge shock is modulated by the defender's readiness at the moment of contact:

**Tier 1 - Instinctive brace (automatic, ANY unit).** A unit qualifies when ALL hold at impact: (a) the charger entered the defender's FRONT edge arc at least `brace_reaction_s` (start 1.5s) before contact while above `charge_min_speed`; (b) the defender is NOT engaged with any other enemy; (c) the defender's own speed <= `brace_max_own_speed` (start 25% of its Speed - standing or shuffling, not marching/running). Effect: charge cohesion shock x `instinctive_brace_mult` (start 0.6). Frontal only - Tier 1 never applies to side/rear contacts (no time to reface; this is why flanks stay lethal).

**Tier 2 - Set to receive (Pierce, existing WO-016 brace).** Unchanged: stationary Pierce facing >= `brace_time_s` negates shock entirely and reflects. Tier 2 supersedes Tier 1 when both qualify.

**Tier 3 - Caught unaware (no brace).** Any unit failing Tier 1's conditions - already engaged, routing, moving above the speed cap, or contacted on side/rear - takes FULL directional shock.

Design consequence (intended): steady infantry that sees a charge coming holds the line; busy, moving, surprised, or outflanked infantry is shattered. Cavalry becomes a maneuver-and-timing weapon, not a delete key.

## Task 3 - Battle-outcome verification (the design promise)

Design promise under test: *cavalry wins by impact, fades in the grind* - a frontal charge that fails to break steady infantry should see the cavalry LOSE the subsequent melee (modest close_damage vs infantry's 25). Report actuals; if cavalry wins the grind too, that's a finding for TD.

## Task 4 - New scenarios (fast mode, evidence logs)
